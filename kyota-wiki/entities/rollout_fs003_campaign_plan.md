## Context: What FIDESZ SAPKA Already Is

The group has a coherent visual/linguistic system already, and the rollout extends it rather than introducing anything new.

**Verified catalog:**
- **FS-001 — "adide"** (2025). Cover: pixelated Google Drive logo on black. Title echoes Hungarian *"add ide"* ("give it here").
- **FS-002 — "kia"** (2025). Cover: photo of three figures huddled around a car at night, flashlight-lit. Title reads as the KIA car brand and lands in everyday Hungarian speech.
- **FS-003 — "király"** (latest release, zero social promotion). Cover: pixelated Google Drive logo on purple. *"Király"* = *king* formally, *sick/cool* in everyday slang.
- **FS-004** — mixed/mastered, blocked on cover art, inherits the next catalog slot.
- **Album (upcoming)** — cover already decided: Google Drive logo on **white**, visibly more pixelated / lower quality than the singles. The endpoint of the visual arc.

**Visual/linguistic through-lines:**
- Flat, familiar Hungarian words as titles — each doubles as slang, a brand, or a speech fragment. Meaning is always dual.
- Lo-fi corporate-logo covers — the Google Drive logo recurs (black → purple → white); FS-002's photo is the one break.
- Progressive degradation across the catalog. Each Drive-logo cover is slightly more pixelated than the previous one, bottoming out on the album's white cover. The visual is being re-saved to death — a file getting recompressed each time someone shares it. The compression arc is itself a narrative thread for the careful fan to notice.
- Deadpan tone baked in. The gap between the grand title ("király" = king) and the mundane icon (a Drive folder logo) IS the joke. Nothing in the covers over-explains.
