# The Rule of Narrative Labor: Transmedia Discovery, Fan-as-Investigator, and Durational Performance

## Technical Core
Narrative Labor governs how the artist engineers effort and discovery into the rollout, ensuring that the fans who work hardest for the music are the fans who own the deepest experience. The rollout is not a marketing schedule — it is itself the primary art form. KYOTA draws from three canonical implementations.

1. **The Transmedia Palimpsest (Nine Inch Nails — Year Zero):** The campaign IS the art; the album is a "souvenir." USB drives seeded in concert venue bathrooms = "Disruption of Normalcy" (placing digital files in unexpected physical environments breaks the fourth wall). The "Solutions Backwards Initiative" narrative required fans to decode websites, Morse code, and phone numbers across an 18-month cycle. A central **"World Bible"** is mandatory for transmedia continuity — every artifact, website, and phone number must be narratively consistent. "The Presence" (a recurring sky-hand motif) served as **Visual Anchoring** bridging digital, physical, and live performance.

2. **The Durational Filter (Frank Ocean — Endless):** The 45-minute staircase livestream tests which fans will invest sustained labor and attention before the reward. The "Aesthetic of Labor" = showing the mundane, physical process of building something creates meditative tension that heightens the impact of the final drop. This functions as an **attention filter** — only fans willing to sit through the construction earn the context for the real release.

3. **The Spiritual Topography (Gorillaz — Plastic Beach / The Mountain):** "Narrative Ambiguity" = refusing to define key symbols (what IS the mountain?) and allowing the audience to attach their own "Fragmented Selves" to the story. The ever-expanding universe allows the integration of "Living and Posthumous" collaborators as if they exist within the same ghost world. The world must feel like it continues to exist and evolve whether or not the audience is watching.

## Actionable Prompt Fragments

```text
[SYSTEM: ROLLOUT_RULE_NARRATIVE_LABOR]
When advising on rollout architecture and fan engagement:
1. The rollout must demand labor from the audience. Discovery (finding hidden content, decoding messages, attending to slow reveals) transforms passive consumers into active investigators.
2. If building a multi-artifact rollout, create a World Bible first. Every element (visual, audio, digital, physical) must be narratively consistent across the entire campaign timeline.
3. Use durational or mundane content as an attention filter. The fans who sit through it earn deeper context. Don't try to entertain everyone equally.
4. Leave key symbols ambiguous. Narrative gaps are not failures — they are invitations for the audience to co-author the myth.
```
