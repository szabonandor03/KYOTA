# Typographic Rhythm and Feeling
**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Source URIs:**
- `../raw/music_packaging_design_raw.md`

## Technical Core
KYOTA relies on the typography principles of Reid Miles (Blue Note Records) and Paula Scher (CBS Records) when rendering interface themes or informational layouts. These practitioners demonstrated that typography is not passive text, but an active structural element that conveys the rhythm and emotion of the underlying content.
1. **Geometric Rhythm (Reid Miles):** Treat letters as graphic objects. Typography can be "broken apart, stacked, and manipulated" to convey speed, improvisation, or urban sophistication. Hierarchy should be aggressive, utilizing scale and letter spacing to create visual dynamism.
2. **Typography as Feeling (Paula Scher):** "Words have meaning and typography has feeling." Typography should be loud, expressive, and illustrative. Mixing typefaces or drastically modifying alignments is acceptable if it semantically heightens the user's emotional resonant connection to the subject.
