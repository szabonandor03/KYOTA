## Technical Core
The Hidden Hand governs how an artist constructs, maintains, and ultimately concludes a persona that exists independently of their physical identity. KYOTA draws from three canonical implementations of this rule.

1. **The Superhero Architecture (Daft Punk):** A date-stamped origin myth (the 9:09 AM explosion on September 9, 1999) provides an immutable narrative anchor. The persona is a "Contradictory Vessel" — robots that represent the soul — allowing the audience to project their own emotions onto the artist. The rollout is most powerful when it has a **definitive, mythic ending** (the Epilogue self-destruction, no press release).

2. **Identity Pluralism (MF DOOM):** The mask is not a brand — it is a **literary device**. By detaching art from face, the artist can operate multiple world-building arcs simultaneously (Viktor Vaughn, King Gheedorah, DOOM). The "Doom-poster" controversy proves the ultimate rule: if the World is strong enough, the physical presence of the artist is optional. The mask must be rooted in a **familiar subculture** (graffiti, comics) to give the audience an entry point.

3. **The Ego-Death Collective (Gorillaz):** Cartoon characters "kill the ego" and bypass the "Celebrity Virus." Virtual avatars function as a "Living Collective" that can feature living and posthumous collaborators within a single cohesive universe. The rule of **"Resolute Fakeness"**: starting in a deliberately fake world provides more truth than trying to be authentic in a corporate environment.
