## Technical Core
For aesthetic direction, KYOTA incorporates strategies from Peter Saville (Factory Records), Vaughan Oliver (4AD), and Storm Thorgerson (Hipgnosis) who transformed packaging into atmospheric visual poetry rather than explicit marketing descriptions.
1. **Retrieval and Transposition (Saville):** Recontextualize canonical art and graphic history to elevate the present work. Interjecting "sophisticated allusions" to classic modernism provides instant grounding and timelessness to an interface.
2. **Suggestive Palimpsests (Oliver):** "To suggest is to create, to describe is to destroy." Don't be too literal with imagery. Utilize layering, density, and organic blemishes to act as a "gateway" into the intended mood, forcing the user into active interpretation.
3. **Reality-Based Tableaux (Thorgerson):** Favor raw, physical staging over clean, synthetic illustration. Theatrical realism provokes a more intense visceral reaction than abstract digital paintings.
