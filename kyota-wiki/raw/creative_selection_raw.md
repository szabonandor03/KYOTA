# Creative Selection
**Author:** Ken Kocienda
**Year:** 2018

## Key Atomic Claims
- Demos are the primary unit of progress; you must start approximating your end goal as soon as possible.
- Taste is the ability to form gut opinions that you can later justify with your head.
- The creative process happens in small, incremental steps that slowly converge on a refined vision.

## Raw Synthesis
The Apple design process is based on an evolutionary model of software development called "Creative Selection." It is anti-theoretical. It rejects standard specification documents in favor of immediate, concrete prototypes. The only way to move a project forward is to build a "demo" that correctly approximates the real interaction, even if hacky. Those demos are then brought together and subjected to critical review by the leadership team. The process relies upon "taste": a highly developed sense of what is good, what fits the product vision, and what feels right. The intersection of inspiration, collaboration, craft, diligence, decisiveness, and taste is what results in revolutionary software, honed by continuous iterative selection.
