# Grid Systems in Graphic Design
**Author:** Josef Mueller-Brockmann
**Year:** 1981 / 1996 (English)

## Key Atomic Claims
- The grid is a fundamental tool for organizing content to achieve visual harmony and logical order.
- Mathematical precision in layout facilitates clarity and objectivity, reducing the influence of subjective "taste."
- The proportions of the page must be mirrored in the divisions of the grid to maintain a coherent visual relationship.

## Raw Synthesis
The use of the grid system implies the will to systematize, to clarify. It is not merely a stylistic tool but a philosophical imperative. By replacing subjective "taste" with an objective, mathematically derived proportion based on the format of the page (for instance, the golden ratio, square, or standard DIN formats), the designer ensures that the audience receives the information with maximum clarity. The text, illustrations, and negative space are all governed by exactly the same dimensional system. By limiting choices to those allowed by the grid, the designer achieves the necessary contrast and tension without dissolving into chaos.
