# The Vignelli Canon
**Author:** Massimo Vignelli
**Year:** 2010

## The Intangibles
* **Semantics:** Understand the subject to convey it meaningfully. Search for the meaning of whatever needs to be designed. 
* **Syntactics:** Control the syntax of the design—the grammar of the elements: structure, grid, typefaces, text, illustrations.
* **Pragmatics:** The design must be understood by the audience to whom it's directed. Clarity of intention equals clarity of result.
* **Discipline:** The underlying force that keeps everything in check. Without discipline, there is no design, just expression.
* **Appropriateness:** The correctness of a specific design for a specific use. 
* **Timelessness:** Design shouldn't follow fads. A good design should last forever.

## The Tangibles
* **Paper:** The basis.
* **Grids:** The grid represents the basic structure of the graphic design. It is the framework that organizes the page.
* **Typefaces:** There are too many typefaces. A few basic ones are sufficient: Garamond, Bodoni, Century Expanded, Helvetica, Times Roman.
* **White Space:** Space is a physical element. White space is not empty; it is active.

## Key Atomic Claims
- Design is a singular discipline; there is no difference between designing a spoon and designing a city.
- Visual power is achieved through the reduction of elements to their most functional and essential forms.
- Appropriateness is the measure of design success—the work must fulfill its purpose without excess.
